Kolhapuri Misal



Ingredients

Vatan
  • Oil - 2 TBSP
  • Masala Mix 1
    • Cumin Seeds - 1 TBSP
    • Shahjeera - 1 TSP
    • Coriander Seeds - 1 TBSP
    • Cinnamon - 0.5 inch
    • Green Cardamom - 2
    • Black Cardamom - 1
    • Black Peppercors - 1 TSP
    • Cloves - 4
  • Mix Number 2
    • Ginger - 0.25 inch
    • Garlic - 10 pieces
    • White Sesame Seeds - 1 TBSP
    • Khuskhus - 1 TBSP
  • Onions - 2 medium-sized
  • Dry Coconut - 0.5 pieces
  • Fresh Grated Coconut - 0.25 cup
Matki Preparation
  • Matki - 60 grams (Dry)
  • Turmeric Powder - 0.25 TSP
  • Salt - To taste
  • Water - grams
Tarri Preparation
  • Oil - 0.5 cup
  • Onion - 1 big-sized
  • Masala Mix 3
    • Kanda Lasun Masala - 2 TBSP
    • Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder - 1.5 TSP
    • Turmeric Powder - 0.5 TSP
Matki Usal
  • Oil - 1 TBSP
  • Mustard Seeds - 1 TSP
  • Green Chillies - 2
  • Matki - Cooked
  • Masala Mix 4
    • Turmeric Powder - 0.5 TSP
    • Kashmiri Red Chilli Powder - 1 TSP
    • Kandha Lasun Masala - 1 TBSP
    • Salt - To Taste
  • Fresh Coriander - 1 handful

Instructions

Vatan
  1. Take a pan and add oil.
  2. Once the oil is medium hot, add Masala Mix 1 and cook for a few seconds.
  3. Add Mix Number 2 and cook for a minute on very low flame.
  4. Transfer this mixture to a bowl and let it cool.
  5. Take onions and make crosscuts (not completely through) without removing skin.
  6. Put a jali on stove and put onions and dry coconut directly on the the jali.
  7. Now let the dry coconut and onions blacken completely.
  8. When it cools down, cut into smaller pieces.
  9. Take a mixer jar and add cooked masala, dry coconut, cooked onions, fresh grated coconut, and a little water.
  10. Make the finest paste possible.
  11. Vatan is ready.
Matki
  1. Take a pressure cooker and add sprouted matki, turmeric powder, salt, and water.
  2. Pressure cook for just 1 whistle.
  3. Strain the matki and save the water which will be used while making misal.
Tarri
  1. Take a large pan and add oil.
  2. Once the oil is hot, add chopped onion and cook for atleast 5-6 minutes o medium flame.
  3. Add Masala Mix 3 and cook for 2 minutes while stirring continously.
  4. Add blended vatan and cook until the oil separates. This process takes minimum 10 minutes. If you feel the masala is getting dry, add a little water and continue cooking.
  5. When the oil is separated, add the water in which matki was cooked.
  6. Now cook for 20-25 minutes on low flame.
  7. Add fresh coriander.
  8. Tarri is ready.
Matki Usal
  1. Take a pan and add oil.
  2. Add mustard seeds and chopped green chilli and let the mustard seeeds crackle.
  3. Add cooked matki, Masala Mix 4 and cook for 5-6 minutes.
  4. Add fresh coriander.
  5. Matki Usal is ready.
Misal
  1. Take bowl and add matki usal, tarri, and farsan.
  2. Garnish with onions and lemon.
  3. Misal is ready.
